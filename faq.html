Frequently Asked Questions about Generative AI

Generative AI is a tool mostly used to assist many people on tasks that can be reduced with its assistance. But some people might not know about of it really works and think as a "human" flawlessly, when in fact it uses different models to create responses that satisfy many users. Bellow this explains many questions about types of AI, how do we properly use it, and its strengths and weaknesses.

The use of Generative AI can help you generate content such as text, images, music, videos, or code at a very fast level. Instead of just identifying or classifying data, these models generate outputs based on patterns learned from large datasets. For example, chatbots, image generators, and code assistant AIs (IBM Think, n.d.; Google, 2023).

Generative AI utilizes machine learning models like neural networks. Machine Learning Model lets AI learn patterns, relationships, and structures from many datasets. As when an AI machine was given a prompt, the model predicts what element will be next to be in the output like Chat GPT which utilizes Large Language Models. Elements like word, pixel, or sound. The model uses statistical probabilities to produce answers that are "similar" to what a human would think (Google, 2023; Stanford CRFM, 2023).

There are many types of Artificial Intelligences that is used for specific scenarios (IBM Think, n.d.; Google, 2023). These examples of AI are:
Text generation (LLMs):
  • ChatGPT
  • Google Gemini/Bard
  • Claude AI
  • Meta LLaMA models
Image generation:
  • DALL·E
  • Midjourney
  • Stable Diffusion
Video generation:
  • OpenAI Sora
  • Runway Gen-2
Code assistants:
  • GitHub Copilot
  • CodeWhisperer
Audio/music:
  • Suno
  • Udio
  • OpenAI Audio models

Generative AI excels at creating drafts quickly for essays and script, Explain details that are easily digestible for many people, create images, videos, and graphics just from prompts, debugging and creating a sample code, brainstorming ideas, personalized tutoring, and language translations. Generative AI is a tool used to solve problems that could take time and effort. Which helps people to focus on other activities where most humans can excel at (Google, 2023; IBM Think, n.d.).

Generative AI has a tendency to produce factually wrong information, bias responses depending on the AI's training data is being fed to the AI program, overreliance of students and even people of AI which results on copyright issues if used inefficiently and weakening of critical thinking, privacy concerns, lack of maintaining memory of past conversations and emotions (UNESCO, 2021; Stanford CRFM, 2023; Google, 2023).
